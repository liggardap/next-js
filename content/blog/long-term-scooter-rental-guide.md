Bali has become one of the world's most popular destinations for digital nomads, remote workers, and long-stay travellers. If you are planning to spend a month — or several months — on the island, renting a scooter long-term is almost always the smartest transport decision you will make.

## What to Look For in a Long-Term Rental Provider

Not all rental operators are equal. Here is what separates a trustworthy provider from a dodgy one.

### Clear Contract Terms
A proper rental agreement should state the daily or monthly rate, deposit amount, what happens in case of an accident, and who is responsible for what type of damage. If an operator refuses to provide a written contract, walk away.

### Maintenance and Breakdown Support
Ask explicitly: what happens if the bike breaks down? A reputable provider will have a support line, will send a mechanic or replacement bike, and will not charge you for normal wear-and-tear repairs.

### Vehicle Age and Condition
Avoid bikes that are more than 3–4 years old unless the provider can show recent service records. Older bikes are more prone to breakdowns, and parts can be harder to source for older models.

### Insurance Coverage
Check whether basic third-party insurance is included. In Bali, most scooter rentals do not include comprehensive coverage — so understand exactly what you are and are not covered for before signing.

## Choosing the Right Bike for a Long Stay

For most long-term riders, an **automatic scooter** in the 110–125cc range (Honda Scoopy, Yamaha Fazzio) is the sweet spot. They are economical on fuel, easy to park, and handle Bali's varied terrain comfortably.

If you plan to do longer rides — to Amed, Munduk, or across to West Bali — consider stepping up to a **150–160cc bike** like the Honda PCX or Yamaha NMAX. The extra power makes a significant difference on hills and longer highway stints.
