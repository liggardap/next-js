Bali is one of those rare destinations where the journey truly is as beautiful as the destination. And there is no better way to experience it than on a scooter, weaving through emerald rice paddies, jungle roads, and coastal cliffs at your own pace.

## Tegallalang Rice Terraces

Just north of Ubud, the Tegallalang Rice Terraces are a must-ride. The winding road up from Sanur takes about 45 minutes and rewards you with some of the most iconic views in all of Indonesia. Arrive early — before 8am — to beat the tour buses and enjoy the morning mist rising over the terraces.

**Riding tip:** Park at the top and walk down into the terraces. The road is steep and narrow in places, so take it slow.

## Uluwatu Temple

Perched on a dramatic clifftop on Bali's southern Bukit Peninsula, Uluwatu is a 90-minute ride from Sanur along the coastal highway. The road hugs the coastline and offers stunning ocean views the whole way.

Stay for the Kecak fire dance at sunset — it is genuinely one of the most spectacular performances you will see anywhere in the world.

## Sidemen Valley

Far fewer tourists make it to Sidemen compared to Ubud, but those who do are rewarded with extraordinary rice terrace views with Mount Agung as the backdrop. The valley road from Klungkung winds through traditional Balinese villages and is perfect for a half-day scooter ride.

## Amed & Jemeluk Bay

For those willing to venture further east, Amed is a quiet fishing village with incredible snorkelling and diving. The ride from Sanur takes about 2 hours through central Bali and over the hills of Karangasem — challenging but absolutely spectacular.

## Practical Tips for Riding in Bali

- **Always wear a helmet.** It is the law and it could save your life.
- **Carry your international driving permit** or an Indonesian SIM.
- **Fill up at Pertamina petrol stations** — avoid the small plastic-bottle roadside sellers where possible.
- **Watch for road conditions** — potholes and loose gravel are common, especially after rain.
- **Ride during daylight hours** — roads are poorly lit at night and dogs are a genuine hazard.
